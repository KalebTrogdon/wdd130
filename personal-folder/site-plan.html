Audience

Cancer? blood clots? We are here to make those a thing of the past. Our technology allows the user to get rid of such things with this small device you can wear. With nanoparticles we can encapsulate or otherwise help to deliver medication directly to cancer cells and minimize the risk of damage to healthy tissue. This has the potential to change the way doctors treat cancer and dramatically reduce the toxic effects of chemotherapy. We are going to grow complex tissues with the goal of one day growing human organs for transplant. Nanotechnology can improve vaccines, including vaccine delivery without the use of needles. We are working to create a universal vaccine scaffold for the annual flu vaccine that would cover more strains and require fewer resources to develop each year. We hope to break the buildup of plaque in arteries. We want to create a nanoparticle that mimics the body’s good cholesterol, known as HDL, which helps to shrink plaque.

NanoTech

Nanoscience and nanotechnology involve the ability to see and to control individual atoms and molecules. Everything on Earth is made up of atoms—the food we eat, the clothes we wear, the buildings and houses we live in, and our own bodies. But something as small as an atom is impossible to see with the naked eye. In fact, it’s impossible to see with the microscopes typically used in a high school science classes. Nanotechnology is already broadening the medical tools, knowledge, and therapies currently available to clinicians. Nanomedicine, the application of nanotechnology in medicine, draws on the natural scale of biological phenomena to produce precise solutions for disease prevention, diagnosis, and treatment.

Q&A

Common Questions that are asked of us are; is what your doing possible? Is this safe? Is this ethical? And some answers to these hard questions are; Yes, this is very possible, only a short time ago we were able to drop nanotech into oils spills to contain and eat the oil thus saving the marine life. If we set the parameters of the machines to eat specific formations of atoms or specific chemical makeups, we could stop cancer or even the common cold. Yes, it is safe we have tested extensively on test animals with zero problems and are now in the midst of human trials through volunteers. We have been able to get rid of cancer cells that were growing and even myxomatosis in rabbits. The simple answer to is it ethical is yes. We believe in upholding morals and values as a company and that is why even though we used testing animals to test the technology we believed that this was the only possibility to bring us the ability to stop horrendous diseases in humans. None of the test subjects that were used would have survived if left untreated.
